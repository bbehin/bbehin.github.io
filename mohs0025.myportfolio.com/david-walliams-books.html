DAVID WALLIAMS CHILDREN’S BOOKS

Persian Edition, published by Gisa Publishing House

Editorial Design Design
Final size: 14×19 cm | Bleed: 5 mm | Offset printing | Black & White
Adapting a bestselling children’s book series for a Persian-speaking audience, this project involved transforming several of David Walliams’ popular titles into a right-to-left reading format while preserving the original pacing, humor, and expressive visual style. These books combine text with black-and-white illustrations, requiring careful attention to layout rhythm, image integration, and readability for young readers.
Each Persian edition was produced for Gisa Publishing House and follows a consistent, chapter-based format that supports both narrative flow and visual engagement. Although printed in black and white, the layout approach ensures a dynamic and accessible reading experience that mirrors the tone and energy of the original English editions.

Master Pages & Layout System​
These books were designed in Adobe InDesign using a consistent master-page system to maintain visual harmony and streamline formatting across multiple titles. The layouts accommodate both text and illustrations while supporting a right-to-left reading direction for Persian typograph.
 A-Master – Main Content Template
Used for the majority of pages, this master includes:
• Running page numbers
• Book title on even pages (right-hand side in RTL layout)
• Chapter title on odd pages (left-hand side)
• Defined text frames and margins for a clean reading experience
• Supports inline illustrations within the text frame

Main Content Template

Main Content Template in use – Awful Auntie

B– Chapter Opening Template
Used specifically for new chapter openings, this master is designed without headers to allow for full attention on the chapter title and opening illustration. It creates visual breathing room and a clear transition between sections. These pages do not include running headers or page titles.
This master-page system ensures a smooth editorial workflow while preserving the engaging and playful tone appropriate for children’s literature.

​​​​​​​Typography & Text Styling
The typography for this edition was adapted to reflect the original book’s playful and expressive tone, while supporting right-to-left Persian text flow. The design uses a clean, readable Persian typeface for the body text, with customized paragraph and character styles to ensure consistency across the entire layout.
Fonts
Main body text is set in a child-friendly Persian typeface optimized for print clarity. The font was chosen for its readability and ability to perform well with illustrations and variable text rhythm.
Occasional words or phrases that appear in exaggerated or stylized ways in the original English version (e.g. oversized, dramatic sound effects) were manually emphasized using larger font size, bold weight, or alternate styling to mirror the tone of the original.
Paragraph Settings
Each text style includes:
• Justified alignment (RTL) with consistent word spacing
• First-line indent, except when the paragraph follows an illustration, chapter title, or extra vertical spacing
• Balanced leading (line spacing) to improve readability for younger readers
• No hyphenation to preserve word integrity in Persian
