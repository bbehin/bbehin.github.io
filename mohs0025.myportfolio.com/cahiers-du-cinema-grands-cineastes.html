Cahiers du Cinéma: Grands Cinéastes

Persian Edition, published by Dibaye Publishing House

Editorial Design
Final size: 17×23 cm | Bleed: 5 mm | Offset printing | CMYK (4-color)
Adapting a renowned French film series for a Persian-speaking audience, this project involved transforming Cahiers du cinéma: Collection Grands Cinéastes (2007) into a right-to-left reading format while preserving the intellectual and visual integrity of the original design. The collection, first published by the iconic French film magazine Cahiers du cinéma, features essays, interviews, and critical writings that explore the artistic legacy of major filmmakers such as Alfred Hitchcock, Ingmar Bergman, Jean-Luc Godard, and others.
The series demanded a deep sensitivity to editorial structure, typographic rhythm, and image-text balance, allowing the translated content to feel both accessible and authentic for Farsi readers.

Master Pages & Layout System​
This book series was designed in Adobe InDesign using a master-page system to ensure consistency and efficiency across volumes. Two custom master pages were created to structure content types:
 A-Master – Main Content Template
Used for the majority of pages, this master includes:
• Running page numbers
• A sidebar column on the outer margin, used for editorial notes as well as small supporting images (such as portraits, stills, or explanatory visuals)
• Defined text frames and consistent margins for a clean RTL (right-to-left) reading experience
• Modular layout for flexible placement of side elements

B–Blue Pages – Feature Articles & Visual Sections

This alternate master is used for visually distinct sections such as essays, film reviews, or biographical features. It includes:
• A full-bleed blue background to visually separate content
• Multi-column grid for dense editorial layouts
• Support for high-contrast image placements and multi-column text wrapping

Both masters are based on a clear typographic and grid system, with paragraph styles and baseline alignment to ensure structure, readability, and a consistent visual rhythm. The design supports margin annotations and extra clarifications specific to the Farsi edition, while maintaining the spirit of the original French series.
Final Section: Biography & Filmography
Each volume concludes with a dedicated section presenting a brief biography and a complete filmography of the featured director. This content is laid out in a clean, structured format to support quick reference and enhance the reader’s understanding of the filmmaker’s career.

​​​​​​​Typography & Text Styling
The layout relies on a consistent system of paragraph and character styles to ensure visual coherence and readability across the series. All text elements were structured using predefined styles in Adobe InDesign, tailored for Farsi right-to-left typography.
Fonts
Main body text is set in a legible Persian typeface optimized for print reading. The font was selected for its clarity in long-form content and its compatibility with tight line spacing without sacrificing readability.
Headings and section titles use a bolder Persian display font in contrasting color (yellow or white) to create a clear visual hierarchy, especially on colored backgrounds like the Blue Pages.

Paragraph Settings
Each text style includes:
• Justified alignment (RTL) with optimal word spacing
• First-line indent (except for the first paragraph after a heading)
• Balanced line spacing (leading) for dense but readable columns
• No hyphenation for Farsi text to maintain word integrity
• Space-before and space-after are used to visually separate paragraphs and sections

Characters and Paragraphs styles

Color & Emphasis
• Body text: black on white pages, white on blue background
• Section titles: high-contrast yellow for emphasis
• Editorial notes and footnotes: slightly smaller font size, often gray or muted for subtlety
• Latin terms or original titles: styled with a specific character style (italic or English font) for clarity
This structured typographic system ensures consistency across all volumes while supporting the editorial needs of a translated edition, such as margin notes, bilingual elements, and explanatory footnotes.
